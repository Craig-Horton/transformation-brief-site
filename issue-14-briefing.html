A closer look  ·  EU Council, final approval, 29 June 2026

The date has been pushed out, your transformation still continues

What's the latest?

The EU AI Act high risk deadline has moved, with stand-alone high-risk systems now expected to apply from 2 December 2027, and high risk AI built into regulated products expected from 2 August 2028. This triggers a question: can your compliance programme slow down? For me, the answer is most definitely no, because although the date has been pushed out, your transformation still continues. Agents are still screening candidates, shaping performance decisions, supporting promotion reviews and recommending who gets seen by a human, the risk is already in the operating model, even if the date / fine has been pushed further away.

Why does it matter?

This week the same agentic HR initiative from issue 13 went back through the AI BVF with the regulatory lens on, and it came back as Fix, leaning towards Stop, held by governance risk, because nothing else should move until this was fixed. The plan it returned was a pre-deployment sequence:

  • Classify the system under the EU AI Act
  • Map every decision that affects a person, including where GDPR Article 22 may apply
  • Design the human-in-the-loop role with genuine override authority
  • Complete the data lineage and model-risk documentation
  • Run the DPIA, remediate the findings, then re-score
The AI BVF regulatory readout from this run. It also calculated the waiting time, roughly EUR 5m to EUR 9m of organisational drag over a 10 week plan window, and that cost still applies even with the deadline pushed out. The reframe question
If the fine is 16 months away, but your agent screens a candidate this afternoon, who owns that decision today? And if no one can be named, what exactly has the delay bought you?
